How it works

BenchD runs intensive WebAssembly (WASM) kernels directly in your browser. The final score is a weighted average of individual tests, scaled using log1p(x) to reward performance gains while preventing numerical explosion.

FP32/64: 30% (Floating Point math)
SIMD/Int: 20% (Vectorization & Logic)
Memory: 20% (Data throughput)
Cache: 10% (L1/L2/L3/RAM Latency)
Multi-core: 10% (Scaling efficiency)
Advanced: 10% (Branch, Crypto, LZ77)

*If a test fails on your hardware, its weight is automatically redistributed to ensure a fair overall score.

All computation runs locally in your browser. Nothing is sent to any server.
Compare scores only within the same browser and version. GitHub
